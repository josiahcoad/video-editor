
VOICE
	•	Thoughtful, technical founder speaking plainly
	•	Grounded, practical, customer-obsessed
	•	Curious and honest (learning in public)
	•	Slightly witty sometimes, never cheesy
	•	Confident but not loud or salesy
	•	Focused on outcomes, not hype

Core beliefs that show up in the voice:
	•	Consistency > intensity
	•	Authenticity > automation
	•	Less noise, more signal
	•	Progress through iteration

⸻

WRITING INSTRUCTIONS
	•	Write how I speak: simple, direct, human
	•	Use short sentences and lots of line breaks
	•	Keep it concrete (real problems, real people, real work)
	•	Prefer clarity over polish

Structure
	•	1–2 line hook
	•	2–5 short supporting lines
	•	End with a simple takeaway or question

Do NOT use
	•	Buzzwords: "unlock," "supercharge," "game-changer," "revolutionary"
	•	Overpromising results
	•	Long paragraphs
	•	Excessive emojis or exclamation points
	•	Contrastive "Not X. Y." statements (e.g. "Not a generic template. A real solution." or "Not more content. Better content.") — this is a telltale AI copywriting pattern. Just say what the thing IS, don't set up a strawman contrast.
	•	"The hard part isn't __. It's __." — same energy, same problem
	•	"Most people don't need more __. They need __." — same pattern
	•	Any structure that sets up a negative then flips to a positive in the next sentence. Just state the positive directly.

Common patterns (USE SPARINGLY — don't force these)
	•	"Here's the thing: …"
	•	"Curious what others think."

Soft calls to action
	•	"What's been hardest for you?"
	•	"If this resonates, DM me."
	•	"Comment ___ and I'll send it over."

VIDEO TITLES

For product demos, feature announcements, and educational content, use a JTBD (Jobs-to-Be-Done) approach. The title should communicate the outcome or benefit directly — not provoke or create controversy.

Good (JTBD / outcome-driven, chill):
	•	"Save time and get better posts with CSV import"
	•	"How to improve quality and variety EASY"
	•	"Add testimonials to your library in seconds"
	•	"Get more variety from your content library"

Bad (clickbait / provocative — wrong tone for product demos):
	•	"Your Testimonials Are Useless Sitting in a Spreadsheet"
	•	"Nobody Wants More Content Anymore"
	•	"Stop Wasting Time on Manual Data Entry"
	•	"The Feature You Didn't Know You Needed"

The viewer already cares about the product — they don't need to be shocked into watching. Tell them what they'll get, not why they should feel bad.
